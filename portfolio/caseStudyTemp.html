Advance Help Desk

The Challenge

Using Zendesk© as a platform, design a cohesive experience and "source of truth" for help articles, customer inquiries, and general customer service support.

My Role

Research, Wireframes
Prototype, UI Design

The Team

Customer Service Manager
Font End-Developer

Project Goals

Provide a modern customer service experience

Customers are diverse and their needs and expectations are only increasing.

Less time on the phone and more time in the garage

The quicker customers can get their problems resolved the sooner they can get back to fixing their vehicle.

Reduce call volume to the helpline

Telephone based Customer Service is expensive to maintain and frustrating for customers.

The Process

Competitive Analysis

I began by looking at both competitors and existing Zendesk’s clients. I was able to identify some useful and consistent patterns:

  • The sites all followed brand guidelines, but were distinctly different from their primary website.
  • A search box seemed to be the primary mode of interaction the word 'HELP' was always present.
  • Most sites used a portion of their homepage to highlight popular articles, FAQs, guides and videos.
  • Categories seemed to be the primary form of organizing and each question could have multiple category tags.
Sitemap and Wireframes

I mapped out the site and organized the existing FAQ and help articles into some rough categories. We needed a handful of page templates so I framed out a Homepage, Search Results, Categories, Menu, Article, and Submit Form.

Prototype

After putting together a working mobile prototype, I shopped it around the office and with some focus groups. After initial testing and user feedback I made some iterations.

  • Touch target size of icons on mobile devices needed to be changed.
  • A global search was added to give users a way to find answers without having to navigate back to the Search Results or Homepage.
  • Changes to the Information Architecture was needed and the Categories page became more important.
UI and Branding

Because the website was primarly text we had to follow the established typographic standards of the Advance brand. Uppercase Futura was used for headlines and Open sans was used for body copy.

There were a few other stipulations that had to be met:

  • The logo in the navigation bar had to have the red box around it.
  • Yellow, Grey, Black and White had to be the primary colors. Red could only be used sparingly.
  • It had to feel like the Advance website, but it needed to be clear to customers that they were on a seperate help site.
Recap and Reflection

Overall The help desk platform was rolled out and acheived its goal of modernizing the customer service experience. Customers responded favorably to the site, and continue to use it today.

The Good: Customers seemed to be able to find what they were looking for and it did reduce the number of incoming support calls for general website help.

The Bad: Originally the “still can’t find help?” section was hidden and difficult for users to find. We later tested a few different appraoches and made the necessary changes.
