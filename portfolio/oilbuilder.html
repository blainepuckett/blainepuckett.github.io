Oil Bundle Builder

The Challenge

Oil Change Specials are the combo meals of aftermarket auto parts. Simply put, itâ€™s a motor oil and an oil filter at a discount. Selling the specials online was difficult due to part compatiblity. Oil filters are not a universal part. Because of how many products and combinations there could be, there was no easy way for customers to create or take advantage of the specials online.

The challenge was to create an experience which would allow the customers to easily select the two products they would need for their combo. Then the system would bundle them and apply the discount on the backend.

My Role

Analysis • Wireframe • Prototype • Testing

Project Goals

Make it as easy to sell an Oil Change Special online as it is in the store

Oil Change Specials are among the most profitable reoccuring purchase customers make.

The Process

User Flow

I created a user flow diagram to understand all the user interactions required to achieve the goal of the bundle builder. After which, it became clear that I would need to map the customer journey into a handful of steps.

Customer Journey Map
Scenario:

Glenn has a Toyota Camry and a Toyota Yaris.
He needs to change the oil on both and wants to save some money buying the oil & filter as a bundle.

Expectation:

Glenn finds the oil change special promotion link
Glenn uses the Bundle Builder to find the right oil & filter combo for each of his vehicles.

Concepts & Wireframes
Concept 1

I mocked up a wireframe to quickly gather feedback from the team on the overall layout and structure. The first concept was an ambitious attempt to do everyting on one long page.

The team provided some excellent feedback:
  • Too long and clumsy on mobile devices
  • Required too much conditional logic for each section
  • Difficult to add a new vehicle to the virtual garage
  • No price or brand filters on the oil section
Concept 2

The second concept was focused on addressing the pain points of the previous approach and trying to create a smoother mobile first experience. Moving away from the one long page approach, to a step by step process proved to be more user friendly. It allowed users to focus on each step and at a glance understand how far along in the process they were.

Key Improvements:
  • Created a persistent step counter element to easily track progress and provide navigation
  • Added a slide out filters drawer for oil types
  • Included fitment and weight informationon the bundle confirmation step
User Testing & Prototype

I conducted usability testing with customers and employees to validate the new approach. I created a script asking users to build various combinations and select between two vehicles. During the sessions, I observed how they interacted with the prototypes and used the feedback to inform further design iteration.

Recap and Reflection

The implementation of the bundle builder allowed customers to purchase oil change specials online for the first time. This contributed significantly to an increase in online sales of motor oil. Additionally, users found the process intuitive and plans were put in motion to create a similar experience for Brakes, Pads, & Rotors.

The team has continued to refine the experience over time.
