Help Desk

The Challenge

Provide a more modern and cohesive online experience for customer help articles, inquiries, and customer service support.

My Role

Analysis • Design • Prototype

Project Goals

Provide a modern customer service experience

Customers are diverse. Their needs and expectations are growing more complex.

Less time on the phone and quicker repairs

The faster customers can get their problems resolved, the sooner they can get back on the road.

Reduce call volume to the helpline

Telephone based customer service is expensive to maintain and frustrating for customers.

The Process

Competitive Analysis

Looking at both competitors and existing Zendesk’s clients, I was able to identify some useful and consistent patterns:

  • A search box seemed to be the primary mode of interaction.
  • Most sites used the bottom section of their homepage to highlight articles, FAQs, and videos.
  • Categories seemed to be the primary form of organizing and each specific question could have multiple category tags.
Sitemap and Wireframes

I created a site map and organized the existing FAQ articles into some rough categories.

After it was determined that the site would need a handful of page templates, I created wireframes of the Homepage, Search Results, Categories, Article, and Contact Form.

Prototype

After putting together a working mobile prototype, I conducted some initial hallway testing around the office, and with a handful of users. Initial testing and user feedback provided insight which lead to a few changes:

  • A global search feature was added to give users a way to find answers without having to navigate back to the Search Results or Homepage.
  • Breadcrumbs were added to make navigation quicker and easier.
  • A persistent link to the advance e-commerce website was needed.
  • A “Call Our Help Line” link was added for customers who preferred traditional telephone support.
UI & Branding

Because the website was primarily text based, we had to follow the established typographic standards of the existing brand. Uppercase Futura was used for headlines and Open Sans was used for body copy.

There were a few other stipulations that had to be met:

  • The logo in the navigation bar had to have the red box around it.
  • Yellow, Grey, Black and White had to be the primary colors. Red cold only be used as part of the logo.
  • It had to feel like the main website, but it needed to be clear to customers that they were on a separate help page.
Recap and Reflection

Overall the help desk platform was rolled out and achieved its goal of modernizing the customer service experience. Customers responded favorably to the site and we continued to monitor and make changes after the site went live.

One change worth noting is in the “still need help section.” Orignally it was hidden slighty to encourage customers to use the search function before making a phone call or sending a support ticket. While initial testing didn’t raise any flags with the placement, after the site went live customers were quite vocal about how difficult it was to find the contact information.
